How does AI text detection work?

This tool analyzes multiple linguistic patterns to estimate whether text was generated by an AI model. It does not use any AI or machine learning itself -- it relies entirely on statistical heuristics that exploit known differences between human and AI writing styles.

Sentence Length Variation: Human writers naturally vary their sentence lengths -- mixing short punchy sentences with longer complex ones. AI models tend to produce more uniform sentence lengths.

Vocabulary Diversity (Type-Token Ratio): Humans use a wider variety of words relative to text length. AI often relies on a narrower vocabulary with more repetition of common terms.

Burstiness: Human writing has "bursts" of complexity followed by simpler passages. AI text tends to maintain a steady, predictable level of complexity throughout.

Filler Phrase Detection: AI models frequently use phrases like "It's important to note," "In today's digital landscape," and "Let's dive in." The density of these phrases is a strong signal.

Transition Word Density: AI overuses transition words like "Furthermore," "Moreover," and "Additionally" to create an appearance of logical flow.

Average Word Length & Syllable Patterns: AI models tend toward medium-length words and avoid both very short colloquial words and very long specialized vocabulary.

Paragraph Uniformity: AI-generated text tends to have paragraphs of similar length, while human writing naturally varies paragraph sizes.
